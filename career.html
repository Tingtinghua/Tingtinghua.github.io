Some Club Career Story

In 2000, he was taken to Barcelona by coach KUKA, and Messi moved his family to Spain. In September, Messi, who is only 13 years old and only 140cm tall, went to Barcelona youth training camp for trial training in ramasia. During the trial period, Messi's performance conquered the Barcelona youth team coach, who signed a contract with Messi that will expire in 2012. Barcelona club has made great efforts to help Messi grow up. As Messi's epiphyseal line has long been closed, club experts have specially developed a series of leg exercises for him to help Messi grow up.


On November 16, 2003, 16-year-old Messi made his first appearance for Barcelona in the friendly against Porto.


In 2012, Messi scored 91 goals, breaking the record of 85 goals set by Germany's gayder Mueller in 1972.


On July 1, 2020, Messi scored his 700th career goal from a penalty kick in the 33rd round of La Liga match between Barcelona and Atletico Madrid.


On December 22, 2020, Messi scored a goal, contributed an assist and broke the previous single club goal record (643 goals) set by Pele for Santos club in the 15th round of Barcelona's away match with baladolide in La Liga.


Some National Team Story

In June 2004, Messi played for the first time on behalf of Argentina's national youth team, playing against Paraguay's U20 youth team.


In 2007, Messi was close to his first national team honor in the Copa America, but only finished second.


In the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games, Messi won the gold medal with the Argentina national Olympic team. DiMaria's winning goal in the final came from his assists. In the World Cup qualifier in South Africa, Messi's performance was not so good, but Argentina finally made the cut.


On March 28, 2009, Messi played against Venezuela in Argentina. For the first time, Messi put on Argentina's No. 10 shirt. This is also the first official game after Maradona became Argentina's coach. The team won 4-0 and Messi opened the record.


On October 9, 2020, in the first round of the South America qualifier of the 2022 Qatar world cup, Argentina won Ecuador 1-0 at home, and Messi scored the penalty made by ocampos, which is also Messi's 71st goal in his national team career.
